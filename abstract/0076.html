Are web surveys better at measuring political attitudes and voting behavior? A comparison between CRONOS web-panel and European Social Survey.
Gina Anghelescu
University of Sydney / Political Economy Department, Sydney

CRONOS panel has the unique feature of a cross-country web panel, which was designed using as ‘sample frame’ the respondents to the European Social Survey (ESS). Three countries participated in CRONOS: the United Kingdom, Slovenia, and Estonia. The aim of this paper is two-fold. It firstly explores the methodological issues related to drawing a web panel from the respondents to a nationally representative face-to-face survey, and then it focuses on how reporting political attitudes and voting behaviour depend on survey mode and question wording. The first part of the paper analyses whether the profile of CRONOS respondents is similar to the one of ESS respondents. Selection probabilities and non-response patterns are studied within- and between- the three countries included in CRONOS. The second part of the paper examines if political attitudes and voting behaviour are reported differently online compared to a face-to-face CAPI interview by CRONOS versus ESS respondents. The paper makes use of the various experiments incorporated in CRONOS survey and tests how gendered views to politics can be revealed by question wording. The paper also screens whether modelling voting behaviour using ESS data can be transposed on data collected from the web panel, when using the same predictors. The third part of the paper discusses the results and the expected implications of using web-surveys in political science. The paper contributes to political methodology literature by providing new insights regarding the challenges of analysing political attitudes and voting behaviour, as captured by online surveys.
